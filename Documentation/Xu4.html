Getting Started

Ultima IV is an involved game, one that changed the world of RPGs forever. It makes use of almost the full keyboard, and it's strongly encouraged that the player download and read the reference manuals via the links above to really get into the game. The game comes with a cloth map (with some parts of the world unknown for the player to explore on his or her own) and a spellbook (with a couple spells unknown and others having easier versions for the player to discover) in addition to a full manual and quick player reference. You'll need all of these to win, and as they're written from the perspective of someone within the fantasy world prevented they're reasonably entertaining on their own.

To get started right away, note that one can get to the initial menu from the title screen by pressing the spacebar, and a menu item can be quickly selected by typing its first letter. You don't get to design your own character; you'll instead be asked a series of questions that take some measure of your personality. Your answers will influence the character that gets created.

Once you're in the game proper you can use the cursor keys to move around and most of the other keys on the keyboard have commands associated with them. The Players Guide has all the details, but a few really useful ones are:

a
Attack.
b
Board a ship or mount a horse.
c
Cast a spell, assuming you've already got one prepared.
e
Enter.
h
Hole up and camp to rest, eat, and hopefully heal.
r
Ready a weapon; okay to use in battle.
s
Searches an area.
t
Talk.
w
Wear armor, takes too long to do in battle, so get ready first.
z
Ztatus; yeah, the s was already used.

Some commands require that certain items are owned, that you're in a certain location, or other special situations. With a little help from the Players Guide and Manual you'll figure these out pretty quickly.

You're going to want to talk to pretty much everyone you can. You can type single words to prompt them for responses. Most people respond to things like name, job, and health. Most people also respond to topical items that you pull from conversations with them, or get told by other people to ask them about.

PocketC.H.I.P. Considerations

This PocketInstaller version of Xu4 makes use of the DOS version of Ultima IV (that has been legally released as freeware) and the U4 Upgrade project (that fixes some bugs and brings the graphics and sound more in line with what was released for Ultima V).
